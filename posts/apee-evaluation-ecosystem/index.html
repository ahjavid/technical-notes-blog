APEE: Adaptive Poly-Agentic Evaluation Ecosystem

A comprehensive framework for evaluating multi-agent AI systems using LLM-as-a-Judge methodology

December 2025 ~15 min read 12 Scenarios Active Research

Overview

The Adaptive Poly-Agentic Evaluation Ecosystem (APEE) is a framework for systematically evaluating multi-agent AI systems. It uses LLM-as-a-Judge evaluation where large language models (20-24B parameters) evaluate smaller agent outputs, providing meaningful, nuanced scores rather than simple heuristics.

Latest Results

Key Findings (Basic Mode)

  • Research synthesis leads: 7.3/10 overall - sequential pattern excels at structured analysis
  • Debate and parallel tie for second: 7.1/10 - constrained_problem, creative_collab, and realtime_collab
  • L2 Collaborative is the bottleneck: Average 5.6/10 vs L1 (5.7/10) and L3 (8.3/10)
  • L3 Ecosystem strongest: All scenarios score 7.6-9.0/10 - system-level metrics consistently high
  • Score range: 5.9-7.3 with mean 6.6/10 across all 12 scenarios
